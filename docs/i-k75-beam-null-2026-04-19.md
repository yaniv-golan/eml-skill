# i witness — beam-search null result at K<=75 (2026-04-19)

## Summary

A 45-minute targeted beam-search for the constant `i` under `max_k=75`,
`per_level_cap=300000`, `seed_witnesses=True`, `seed_subtrees=True`, and
`domain=complex-box` returned **no match**. The shipped K=91 (nine-stage
pi/i construction) upper bound therefore stands. The paper's direct-search
floor of >55 is not refuted, but neither is it closed.

## Command

```
PYTHONPATH=eml-skill/skills/_shared \
  python eml-skill/skills/eml-optimize/scripts/optimize.py search \
    --target i --max-k 75 --time-budget 2700 --per-level-cap 300000 \
    --domain complex-box --seed-witnesses --seed-subtrees --format json
```

Symbolic gating was intentionally skipped: `i` is not registered in
`eml_core.symbolic.SYMBOLIC_TARGETS` (which covers the closed-form
elementary targets — `neg`, `inv`, `exp`, `ln`, `e`, `add`, `sub`, `mult`,
`pow`), so the `--symbolic-gate` flag would have no effect for this target.

## Outcome

- **Found:** `false`
- **Stopped reason:** `time-budget` (2732.1 s wall-clock against a 2700 s
  budget — the process returned on the next level boundary after the timer
  tripped).
- **Candidates evaluated:** 1,900,533 unique function vectors.
- **Best K:** `n/a`.

At K=17 through K=27 the `per_level_cap=300000` saturated — meaning
bottom-up enumeration was still discovering genuinely novel vectors faster
than the budget could admit. The sharp drop at K>=29 reflects the search
running out of wall-clock before level K=29's enumeration could widen; the
handful of entries at K=29..65 are seed-level contributions (pre-populated
library subtrees from sqrt K=59 and pi K=137's subtree decomposition).

## Interpretation

1. **The K=75 cap is not restrictive enough.** The combinatorial
   explosion between K=17 and K=27 consumed the entire 45-minute budget;
   the search never reached its K=75 ceiling via bottom-up enumeration.
   Higher K levels contain only the seeded subtrees, not newly-composed
   candidates.

2. **Seeded pool did not hit.** Even with every library witness subtree
   pre-installed at its natural K, the generalized meet-in-the-middle
   scan (`_generalized_targeted_scan`) produced no match for `i`. The
   existing K=91 nine-stage construction has no obvious structural
   shortcut through sqrt/neg/exp/ln/pi building blocks at K<=75.

3. **Paper K>55 floor is consistent.** Our empirical floor at
   `per_level_cap=300000` is >=75 (i.e. the search cannot exhibit a K<=75
   witness for `i` under these parameters). The paper's reported
   direct-search lower bound of >55 therefore still sits comfortably
   below our upper bound of 91; the gap 56..91 is unresolved.

## No cascade

Because no K<75 witness was discovered, the existing i tree (K=91) is
unchanged, and all dependents (`pi`, `sin`, `cos`, `tan`, `asin`, `acos`,
`atan`, and the three internal `_complex_box` variants implied by the
proof-engine construction) keep their current K values. No mutation to
`witnesses.py`, `tests/test_witnesses.py`, or `docs/leaderboard.md` is
required.
